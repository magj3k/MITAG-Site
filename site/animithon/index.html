What is ?

AniMIThon is the MIT Animation Group's first ever animation competition. Teams from MIT and other locations from across the United States can compete for prizes and the chance for their film to be shown in local theaters*.

Join by yourself or with a team and submit your film by early April for the chance to win from over $500 of prizes including tablets and projectors.

•

No prior experience is necessary; only a passion for learning & exploring is required! Participate in any of our MITAG events/workshops throughout the semester to learn more about animation for your project.

For more information about joining the competition or key dates, check out the sections below or feel free to contact us with any questions. Happy animating!

Important Dates & Events

Timeline:
March 4, 2018 - AniMIThon Officially Begins
 • Teams can begin work on animated film projects.
April 5, 2018 - Check-In Deadline
 • All teams must complete a checkoff (approved my MITAG staff) to be eligible to receive awards. Check-in information will become available by the end of March. Applications to join AniMIThon will also be closed, and film submissions will be opened.
April 21, 2018 - Film Submission Deadline
 • All active teams must submit their final films by this date. For submission information, check out the Submission Instructions & Requirements section.
April 28, 2018 - Awards Ceremony (Tentative)
 • Teams will be invited to a live awards presentation at MIT.

Check-In Submissions


To be eligible to receive prizes, all teams must submit a snippet of their final film projects and submit our Check-In form by April 5th:

 Check-In Form CLOSED

All Check-In film snippets must follow the following guidelines:

•  Be at least 5 seconds long
•  Demonstrate animation in some form (stop-motion, traditional, etc.)

Each team will receive a submission confirmation email after submitting the Check-In form.
Submission Instructions & Requirements

 CLOSED

Final animated film submissions must:

•  Be at least 30 seconds long
•  Only contain original, royalty-free, or public domain assets and characters
•  Demonstrate some form of animation throughout most of its runtime
