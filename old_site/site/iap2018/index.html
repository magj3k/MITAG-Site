Assignments
Assignment #1

Description:

Think of a relatively simple object, like a flour sack or magic carpet. Brainstorm and sketch out some possible emotions this object could portray. Then, choose one emotion, and make a short flipbook animation that shows this object enacting this emotion--for instance, a flour sack waving hello excitedly, or a carpet dragging itself along the floor like an inchworm dejectedly. Make sure to emphasize your use of squash & stretch.

Take a video and submit online by the start of class on 1/17. More detailed submission instructions below.
Assignment #2

Description:

Create your own character and imagine how they would interact with a weighted cube or ball. Using Tupi (or another animation program), first animate the motion of the weighted object using tweens, then animate the character interacting with the object on a separate layer.

Upload to a video sharing website of your choice and submit by the start of class on 1/24. More detailed submission instructions below.
Assignment #3

Description:

Create a short stop-motion film using clay models (or a modeling substitute, like Lego) and illustrated backgrounds. First, plan out your film with a storyboard. Then, take pictures for each frame in the film and splice them together in a video editor of your choice. Add sound effects (and optionally music), then export as a video file and submit.

If you're having a rough time finding out where to start, feel free to use this prompt as inspiration:

A mad scientist is hard at work experimenting in their lab, until something goes wrong.

Upload to a video sharing website of your choice and submit before 1/31. More detailed submission instructions below.
